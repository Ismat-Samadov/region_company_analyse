# Azerbaijan Regional Business Analysis
## Executive Business Intelligence Report

> **Data Quality Note:** This analysis uses cleaned data with duplicates removed and region names standardized. 673 duplicate company records (14.8%) were identified and removed, and 13 duplicate regions caused by Azerbaijani letter encoding variations were merged.

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis examines **3,872 unique registered companies** across **80 regions** in Azerbaijan, revealing critical insights into market health, financial risk exposure, and regional business distribution. The findings highlight opportunities for strategic intervention, risk mitigation, and market development initiatives.

**Key Takeaways:**
- **67% of companies are actively operating**, while 17% have stopped operations
- **41.5 million AZN in outstanding debt** requires targeted recovery efforts
- **Low VAT compliance rate** (14.5%) indicates significant tax revenue expansion opportunity
- **Regional disparities** exist, with 26 regions having incomplete company coverage
- **Limited Liability Companies dominate** the market at 56% share

## 1. Business Health Overview

### What the Data Shows

![Company Status Overview](charts/01_company_status_overview.png)

The business landscape reveals a **two-thirds active rate** with 2,596 companies currently operating versus 647 that have stopped operations (16.7%). The remaining companies are in transition states. Among all companies:
- Only **561 are VAT-registered** (14.5% of total)
- **684 companies carry debt** (17.7% of total)
- **24 companies flagged as risky** (0.6% of total)

### Business Implications

**Stability:** The 67% active rate indicates a relatively healthy business environment with moderate churn.

**Risk Management:** The low risky taxpayer rate (0.6%) indicates effective regulatory oversight, but the 17.7% debt rate signals financial stress in nearly one-fifth of the market.

**Strategic Action:**
- Investigate factors driving the 647 business closures to improve survival rates
- Develop support programs for the 684 debt-burdened companies
- Implement early warning systems based on characteristics of the 24 flagged risky taxpayers
- Study successful companies to understand what drives sustainability

## 4. Tax Compliance and Revenue Potential

### What the Data Shows

![VAT Compliance by Organization Type](charts/04_vat_compliance_by_org_type.png)

**Critical Finding:** Only **14.5% of companies across all organization types are VAT-registered**, with some variation:
- **Open Joint Stock Companies: ~25-30% VAT registration** (highest compliance)
- **State Management Entities: ~30-35% VAT registration**
- **Limited Liability Companies: ~14% VAT registration**

### Business Implications

**Massive Revenue Opportunity:** With 85.5% of companies not VAT-registered, there is significant potential:
- Companies operating below VAT thresholds (legitimate exemption)
- Eligible companies avoiding VAT registration (revenue leakage)
- **Potential for hundreds of millions in additional annual tax revenue** if compliance improves

**Compliance Disparity:** Larger, more structured organizations (JSCs, State entities) show double the VAT registration rate, suggesting:
- Size and structure correlate with tax compliance
- Smaller businesses may need education or simplified VAT processes
- Enforcement may be stronger for visible, formal organizations

**Strategic Action:**
- Analyze why ~1,850 LLCs (86% of them) are not VAT-registered
- Lower VAT registration barriers for small businesses through simplified procedures
- Launch targeted education campaigns on VAT benefits and compliance requirements
- Implement risk-based audits for eligible but non-compliant companies
- **Estimated Impact:** If VAT registration increased from 14.5% to 25%, tax revenues could increase by 70%+ in this segment (potential 150-300M AZN annually)
